The 1990s and 2000s saw the steepest acceleration. 2010 was the single biggest year — 5 countries elected their first female leader. Today 87 countries have been led by a woman at some point; 106 never have.

WEF Global Gender Gap Index — political empowerment over time
Political empowerment gap closed (%), 2006–2025
From WEF Global Gender Gap Index · 100 constant economies · score = % of political gender gap closed
Political empowerment Overall index Educational attainment Economic participation
Political empowerment has seen the most absolute gains since 2006 (+9pp) but remains by far the most unequal dimension — only 23.4% closed. At current pace, 162 more years are needed to close the political gap alone.
Source: WEF Global Gender Gap Report 2025
